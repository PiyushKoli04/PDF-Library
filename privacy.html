Your Privacy Matters

Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters to us. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

Short version: We keep it minimal. We do not sell your data. We do not show ads. The platform is largely stateless and uses only what's needed to provide the service.

Section 01

Overview

This Privacy Policy describes how Syntax Syndicate ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you use Syntax Archives ("the Service").

We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. If you have any questions or concerns, please contact us.

Section 02

What We Collect

We collect information in two ways: information you provide directly, and information collected automatically.

Scroll to see full table

Data Type How Collected Purpose
Login credentials (username) Entered by user Authentication for premium access
Session data Automatically via localStorage Maintaining login state between pages
Browser & device info Automatically via browser APIs Providing a responsive experience

We do not collect sensitive personal information such as payment details, government IDs, or biometric data.

Section 03

How We Use Your Data

Information we collect is used strictly to operate and improve the Service:

  • To authenticate users and manage access to premium content.
  • To maintain your session so you don't need to log in repeatedly.
  • To respond to your messages and support requests.
  • To monitor and analyze usage patterns to improve platform performance and content.
  • To protect the platform against abuse, fraud, and unauthorized access.

We will never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

Section 04

Data Storage & Security

Syntax Archives is a primarily static platform. Most session data is stored locally in your browser's localStorage — it never leaves your device unless you interact with a form or backend service.

Where server-side data is involved (e.g., Firebase for authentication), we rely on industry-standard security practices provided by Google Firebase. We implement appropriate technical safeguards to prevent unauthorized access.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Section 05

Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may collect data as part of their operation:

  • Google Firebase — Backend services including authentication and data storage. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Google Fonts — Font loading. Google may log font requests. Subject to Google's Privacy Policy.
  • Bootstrap Icons (jsDelivr CDN) — Icon delivery. Standard CDN request logging may apply.

We encourage you to review the privacy policies of these third-party providers. We are not responsible for their privacy practices.

Section 06

Cookies & Local Storage

We use localStorage (not traditional cookies) to store your authentication session. This data:

  • Remains on your device and is not transmitted to our servers.
  • Is cleared when you log out or clear your browser's storage.
  • Is used only to keep you logged in between page visits.

We do not use tracking cookies, advertising cookies, or any analytics cookies that profile your behavior across other websites.

Section 07

Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Deletion: Request that we delete your personal data.
  • Portability: Request your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection: Object to how we process your data in certain circumstances.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us. We will respond within 30 days.

Section 08

Children's Privacy

Syntax Archives is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will promptly delete it.

Section 09

Policy Updates

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

Section 10

Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy or our data practices, please reach out:

Visit our Contact page or learn more at syntaxsyndicate.co.in. We aim to respond within 48 hours.
